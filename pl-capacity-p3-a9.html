Audit 9 Context
Why This Audit Matters

The measure of professional capacity is not how much goes in — it is what comes out. Throughput is the quality of the conversion between capacity invested and value produced. A professional can be fully occupied, genuinely working hard, and still producing below their structural potential if the work consuming their capacity is not the work where their contribution is most distinctive, most difficult to replace, or most consequential.

This audit examines the quality of your professional throughput: whether your capacity is being deployed in the work that produces its highest return, and whether the output your function generates is commensurate with the capacity it consumes.

Audit 9 Diagnosis
Throughput Diagnosis

Eight dimensions of throughput diagnosis — output-to-capacity ratio, high-value work proportion, distinctive contribution clarity, replacement difficulty assessment, output quality measurement, capacity deployment alignment, throughput optimisation readiness, and contribution return mapping.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Throughput has not been examined. The professional function is operating — work is being done, outputs are being produced — but whether that work is producing the highest professional return on capacity invested has not been questioned.

This is a structural omission rather than a performance failure. Most professionals focus on the volume of output rather than its strategic quality. The question this audit asks — is your capacity producing at its potential, in the work where your contribution is most distinctive? — is not routinely asked. The answer, when examined, often reveals significant structural redeployment opportunity.

What to do this week

Identify the three outputs your professional function produced in the past month that were most difficult for anyone else to produce. These are your highest-throughput outputs — the work where your contribution is most distinctive. Now calculate the percentage of your total weekly capacity deployed producing those categories of output. The ratio is your throughput quality baseline.

Within 14 days

Identify the three categories of work consuming the most capacity in your professional function that could be produced by someone else — with reasonable development, delegation, or structural adjustment. Those categories are your throughput displacement targets: the capacity they consume is the capacity available for redeployment to your highest-distinctive-return work.

Within 30 days

Make one structural change to redirect capacity from a low-distinctiveness, high-volume work category toward a high-distinctiveness, high-return one. This is not about working harder — it is about structural redeployment. The same total capacity, applied differently, produces disproportionately higher professional value.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Throughput quality is partially examined and partially optimised. Some high-value, high-distinctiveness work is occurring. The gap is in precision — the capacity not deployed on highest-distinctiveness work is not clearly identified or structurally targeted for redeployment. Throughput improvement is happening at the margin rather than by architectural design.

What to do this week

Map your current professional output against two axes: value produced (how much does this output matter for the outcomes your function exists to produce?) and distinctiveness (how difficult would it be for another person or resource to produce this output at equivalent quality?). The upper-right quadrant is your highest-throughput work. What percentage of your capacity currently produces work in that quadrant?

Within 14 days

Identify the specific structural mechanism most preventing capacity concentration in your highest-throughput quadrant. Is it the volume of lower-distinctiveness work that fills the schedule? Is it the absence of delegation structure? Is it role boundary insufficiency? That mechanism is your structural throughput target.

Within 30 days

Implement one structural change that increases the proportion of capacity deployed in your highest-throughput quadrant by a measurable amount. Track the output quality of that quadrant over 30 days. The improvement in depth, distinctiveness, and value produced is the measurable return on the structural redeployment.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Throughput is well-diagnosed and capacity is concentrated at its highest-return use. The professional function is producing at a high proportion of its structural potential. The opportunity at this level is in throughput ceiling expansion — identifying the structural investments in capability, relationship, and environment that would raise the ceiling of what your professional function can produce.

What to do now

Identify the single structural investment — in a capability, a relationship, an environment, or a body of knowledge — that would most significantly raise the ceiling of your professional throughput. Not an improvement to current performance, but an expansion of the structural upper limit of what your function can produce. That investment is the highest-return use of the discretionary capacity your optimised architecture has created.
