Phase 2 Context
Why This Matters Now

Phase 2 is Measurement. Expertise that cannot be measured cannot be strategically positioned or protected.

Awareness established what expertise exists. Measurement now determines what it is worth, how it is credentialled, whether it is defensible, and how clearly it is positioned in the minds of those who should be seeking it. Most professionals undervalue their expertise precisely because they have never measured it — converting a felt sense of competence into a calculable professional asset.

Phase 2 concludes by examining the outer expression of your expertise architecture: whether the capability you hold is positioned clearly and deliberately in the professional context where it creates the highest value, or whether it is invisible to those who need it most.

Audit 6 Context
Why This Audit Matters

Expertise positioning is not marketing. It is the deliberate communication of professional capability to the specific audience most likely to value and require it. A professional with exceptional expertise and poor positioning will be perpetually underutilised — sought for work below their capability ceiling, priced below their expertise value, and known to fewer people than the quality of their work warrants.

Most professionals rely on reputation by proximity — they are known to those who have worked with them directly. Expertise positioning extends that recognition deliberately, structurally, and into markets that proximity alone would never reach.

Audit 6 Diagnosis
Expertise Positioning

Eight dimensions of expertise positioning — target audience clarity, positioning statement precision, communication consistency, channel appropriateness, differentiation visibility, reputation scope, positioning measurement, and market recognition reach.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Expertise is not deliberately positioned. You are known in your immediate professional network. You produce good work. But the specific expertise that represents your highest professional value has not been communicated in a way that creates recognition beyond those who have worked with you directly.

The structural consequence is constrained demand. The professionals and organisations who most need your specific expertise — and who would pay most willingly for it — are not finding you, because the signals that would guide them to you have not been deliberately placed. The expertise exists. The communication architecture does not.

What to do this week

Identify your target audience for expertise positioning: the specific professional context — a sector, an organisational type, a professional community — in which your expertise produces its highest value and would be most sought after if it were visible. This is your primary positioning audience. Everything built in the following steps is built for them.

Within 14 days

Write your expertise positioning statement: one to two sentences that describe who you serve, what expertise you bring, and what specifically changes for those who access it. This is not a tagline — it is a structural description of your professional value proposition. It should be specific enough to be immediately relevant to your target audience and immediately non-obvious to a general observer.

Within 30 days

Deploy the positioning statement in three professional touchpoints: your professional biography, your primary communication channel, and your next direct professional introduction. Consistency across touchpoints is the structural mechanism that converts a statement into a market reputation. A positioning that appears in one place is a preference; a positioning that appears consistently across all places is a structural reality.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Expertise is partially positioned. Some relevant professionals are aware of your capability. The gap is reach and precision — the positioning does not extend systematically beyond direct relationships, and in the professional contexts where it does appear, it may not be differentiated clearly enough to stand apart from adjacent competitors.

What to do this week

Audit your current positioning across every professional touchpoint where it appears. Is the language consistent? Is the expertise described with sufficient precision to distinguish it from generic professional descriptions? Is the differentiation — what makes your expertise specifically and structurally different — clearly stated? Inconsistency and generic description are the two most common reasons positioning does not convert awareness into engagement.

Within 14 days

Identify the professional touchpoint where your expertise positioning most frequently reaches your target audience — the context where the people most likely to need your capability encounter your professional presence. Invest the most concentrated positioning effort in that single touchpoint before extending to others.

Within 30 days

Measure the reach of your current positioning: how many professionals in your target audience are aware of your specific expertise? This number is your positioning baseline. Every structural improvement to your positioning architecture should produce a measurable increase in this number.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Expertise positioning is clear, consistent, and reaching the intended audience. The professional market that most needs your capability knows you exist. The opportunity at this level is in positioning precision — moving from accurate positioning to positioning that is so specifically calibrated to the target audience's most urgent needs that it produces immediate recognition and pull.

What to do now

Test the pull-through of your current positioning. When a professional in your target audience encounters your expertise positioning, what percentage of those encounters result in an exploratory conversation? If that conversion rate is below your expectation, the positioning is reaching the right people but not resonating at the depth that would convert awareness into demand. The precision of the positioning — how specifically it addresses the most urgent professional concern of the target audience — is the lever.
