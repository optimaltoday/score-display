Audit 9 Context
Why This Audit Matters

Licensing is the highest-leverage income model available to a knowledge business.

In direct delivery, your income is bounded by your time. Every hour you deliver generates income once. In licensing, your expertise generates income repeatedly — each licensee pays for access, and that access operates independently of your time. The knowledge investment is made once. The return compounds.

Most ASEAN family businesses have never considered licensing as a commercial model. Their expertise is too embedded in their operations to be seen as a separable product. This audit is the diagnostic that changes that perspective — by mapping the licensing potential that already exists within the business's current knowledge assets.

Audit 9 Diagnosis
Licensing Potential

Eight dimensions of licensing potential — licensable asset identification, target licensee profile clarity, licensing model selection, pricing framework, quality control mechanism for licensees, legal structure readiness, market demand evidence, and competitive licensing landscape.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Your business has not assessed its licensing potential. The expertise, systems, and methodologies that have commercial licensing value have not been identified as licensable products, have not been assessed for market demand, and have not been structured into a commercial licensing offering.

This means an entire income category — one that could generate revenue without your direct delivery time — remains at zero. Not because the potential is absent, but because the structural examination has never been done.

What to do this week

Identify three things your business does that another business in your sector would benefit from being able to do. Those are your licensing candidates. For each: is the approach documented? Is the methodology replicable? Would a competitor or adjacent business pay for access to it? The answers define your initial licensing product shortlist.

Within 14 days

Select your most licensable asset and define its commercial form. Is it a licensed methodology? A certified training program? A branded operational system? A franchise model? The form determines the licensing structure, the pricing approach, and the target licensee profile.

Within 30 days

Approach one potential licensee informally. Not with a formal licensing proposal — with a conversation. What would access to your methodology be worth to their business? What would they pay for it? What concerns would they have? Market intelligence from one conversation is worth more than months of internal analysis.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Licensing potential is recognized but not yet structured. You understand that your expertise could be licensed. The commercial architecture — the specific product, the target licensee, the pricing, the legal structure — has not been built.

What to do this week

Define your licensing product with specificity. Not your expertise in general — one specific methodology, framework, or system that can be licensed as a distinct commercial offering. Give it a name. Define what it includes. Define what a licensee receives. The specificity is what makes it a product rather than a concept.

Within 14 days

Build your target licensee profile. Who specifically would benefit most from your licensed product? In what sectors? In what markets? At what business scale? A specific licensee profile makes commercial outreach possible. A general description makes it impossible.

Within 30 days

Draft a simple licensing term sheet. What does the licensee receive? What do they pay? What are the usage restrictions? What are the quality standards they must meet? Even a rough term sheet forces the commercial clarity that converts a concept into a deal-ready offering.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Your licensing potential has been assessed and at least partially structured. A licensable product exists or is close to existing. A target licensee profile has been defined. The opportunity at this level is in execution — moving from a licensing concept to a licensing agreement.

What to do now

Identify your three highest-probability licensing targets — the specific businesses most likely to be first licensees. Approach each with your structured offering. The first licensing agreement will require more effort than subsequent ones, but it will also produce the commercial proof of concept that makes all subsequent licensing conversations easier.
