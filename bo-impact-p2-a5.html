Phase 2 Context
Why This Matters Now

Phase 2 is Measurement. Audit 4 measured pricing authority. Audit 5 examines the foundation of pricing authority: competitive differentiation.

Differentiation is the IMPACT Pillar's most commercially significant element. Without genuine differentiation, brand consistency and clear positioning produce a well-packaged commodity. With genuine differentiation, every other element of the IMPACT Pillar compounds into a commercial moat that competitors cannot easily cross.

Audit 5 Context
Why This Audit Matters

The difference between better and only is the difference between competing and leading.

A business that is better than its competitors competes on merit — and merit is evaluated differently by every buyer. A business that is only — the only one with this specific approach, this specific track record, this specific access — does not compete. It selects. The right clients seek it out. The wrong clients self-select out. And pricing reflects scarcity rather than comparison.

This audit examines whether your differentiation is at the better or only level — and what would need to be true for it to reach only.

Audit 5 Diagnosis
Competitive Differentiation

Eight dimensions of competitive differentiation — differentiation specificity, competitor claim overlap, defensibility of claims, uniqueness of approach or method, client switching cost, differentiation documentation, differentiation communication consistency, and market awareness of differentiation.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Your differentiation is generic. The claims you make about your business — your experience, your reliability, your service quality — are claims that most of your competitors make with equal legitimacy. In a buyer's evaluation, generic differentiation produces a coin-flip decision at equivalent prices and a price-driven decision when prices differ. Neither outcome serves your business.

What to do this week

List every differentiation claim your business currently makes. For each: could a direct competitor make the same claim honestly? If yes — it is not differentiation. It is a baseline expectation. Remove it from your differentiation architecture and replace it with something that only you can claim.

Within 14 days

Identify your genuine differentiator. This is the one element of your business that competitors cannot honestly replicate: a proprietary method, a specific access or relationship, a documented outcome track record, a unique combination of capabilities. Identify it, name it specifically, and build your positioning around it.

Within 30 days

Test your genuine differentiator in a client conversation. Present it as the primary reason to choose you — before presenting your experience, your reliability, or your service quality. Observe the response. A genuine differentiator produces immediate recognition from the right client. If the response is muted, the differentiator needs further refinement.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Some genuine differentiation exists. You have elements of your offering that competitors cannot honestly replicate. The gap is in the communication — those elements are not consistently presented as your primary commercial differentiator, which means they are not producing the pricing authority and market selectivity they are capable of producing.

What to do this week

Identify your strongest genuine differentiator and make it the first thing you communicate in every commercial interaction — before experience, before credentials, before service description. The differentiator should precede everything else. It is the reason the conversation is worth having.

Within 14 days

Document your differentiation evidence. What specific outcomes, track records, or client results prove your differentiation is real rather than claimed? Evidence converts a differentiator from a positioning statement into a commercial proof — and proof is what eliminates negotiation.

Within 30 days

Create a competitive comparison narrative. Not a chart that shows you winning on every dimension — a clear, honest articulation of specifically who you are right for and who you are not right for. That narrative disqualifies the wrong clients before they waste your time and positions you as the only correct choice for the right ones.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Your differentiation is genuine and communicated. The right clients understand why you are the correct choice. Competitors cannot easily replicate what makes you distinctive. The opportunity at this level is in protection — ensuring your differentiation remains genuinely yours as competitors observe what works and attempt to approximate it.

What to do now

Protect your differentiation. Document it formally. If it is a methodology or approach, give it a name and consider IP registration. If it is a track record, systematize the evidence collection. If it is an access or relationship, deepen it. Differentiation that is not actively maintained is differentiation that erodes.

Audit 6 — Internal Authority
Do your team members interpret your direction consistently — and does their interpretation affect client experience?

Competitive differentiation audit examined how you are perceived externally. Internal authority audit examines how your direction is interpreted internally — whether staff, family members, and partners communicate and behave in ways that reinforce or undermine your external positioning.

Internal authority gaps are one of the most damaging and least visible sources of brand incoherence. They are also one of the clearest succession risk signals.
