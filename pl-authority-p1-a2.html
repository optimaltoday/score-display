Audit 2 Context
Why This Audit Matters

A professional position is the specific space a practitioner occupies in the professional market: the combination of expertise, audience, and outcome that makes them the clear choice for a specific category of professional need. A clear position is not a job title — it is a structural declaration of professional specificity. It answers three questions simultaneously and precisely: what expertise do you bring, who specifically do you serve, and what changes for them when they access your work?

Most professionals operate with a position that is too broad to be memorable, too general to generate immediate recognition, or too conventional to differentiate. This audit examines the precision and distinctiveness of your professional positioning statement.

Audit 2 Diagnosis
Positioning Clarity

Eight dimensions of positioning clarity — position statement precision, target audience specificity, differentiation sharpness, memorability, immediate comprehension rate, positioning consistency, professional distinctiveness, and positioning communication effectiveness.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Your professional position is not clearly defined. You occupy a professional function — you perform a role, you deliver work — but the specific position that makes you the clear and preferred choice for a particular category of professional need has not been architecturally constructed or communicated.

The structural consequence is invisibility to the right audience. A professional without a clear position is a professional who is discovered only through proximity — through direct referral, through chance encounter, through the professional network of those who already know them. Clear positioning is the mechanism by which the right audience finds the right professional without requiring that proximity.

What to do this week

Draft your positioning statement this week. It has three components: your professional identity (who you are professionally), your specific audience (who specifically you serve), and your distinctive outcome (what specifically changes for them when they access your work). Draft all three components separately and then combine them into one sentence. Do not aim for elegance on the first pass — aim for specificity.

Within 14 days

Test your draft positioning statement with five people outside your immediate professional circle — people who do not already know your work. Ask them to repeat back to you, in their own words, what you do and for whom after reading or hearing your statement once. The precision of their repetition tells you whether the statement is communicating what you intend.

Within 30 days

Refine the statement based on the testing and deploy it consistently across your primary professional touchpoints. A positioning statement that appears once is an experiment. A positioning statement that appears consistently everywhere is a structural professional signal — and it is the accumulated consistency of that signal, over many encounters and many months, that converts it into a market position.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Your positioning is partially clear. Those who encounter your professional presence in familiar contexts understand what you do. The gap is in unfamiliar contexts — professional introductions, written communications with people who do not know you, digital encounters with potential clients or referrers — where the position requires explanation that the clarity of a well-constructed positioning statement would make unnecessary.

What to do this week

Record your professional introduction the next time you give it — in a meeting, a networking context, or a professional event. Listen to the recording. Does it communicate your position with the same precision and immediacy as your best written description of yourself? If not, the oral positioning architecture is the gap.

Within 14 days

Write the one-sentence positioning statement that you want to use in every professional introduction going forward. Not the description of your function — the declaration of your position. Practice delivering it until it sounds natural rather than rehearsed. The goal is a positioning statement so clear and well-practised that it communicates your position in one sentence without requiring follow-up questions to understand.

Within 30 days

Assess the response your positioning statement generates in three different professional contexts — an industry peer context, a potential client context, and a professional introduction context. Does it generate the same recognition across all three, or does it land differently depending on the context? The differential response tells you which aspect of the positioning needs calibration for specific audience contexts.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Positioning clarity is architecturally achieved. The professional position is stated with precision and immediately understood across contexts. The opportunity at this level is in positioning evolution — examining whether the current positioning continues to accurately represent the highest-value professional contribution you are now capable of making, or whether the ceiling of your expertise has moved ahead of the positioning that describes it.

What to do now

Compare your current positioning statement against the expertise inventory and methodology documentation from the CAPABILITY Pillar. Does the positioning accurately reflect the depth, distinctiveness, and value of the expertise you now hold? Or has the expertise evolved beyond the positioning — a common pattern in high-development professionals where capability grows faster than communication? If so, the positioning needs to be elevated to match the ceiling of what the expertise can now deliver.
