Phase 1 Context
Why This Matters Now

Phase 1 is Awareness. Before anything can be corrected, it must be seen.

The IMPACT Pillar addresses the coherence of how your business presents itself — to customers, to staff, to suppliers, to investors, and to the market. Brand incoherence is both a Money Leak and Money Left simultaneously. It compresses pricing power on every transaction. It makes the business invisible to international capital and premium buyers. It creates internal authority gaps that compound over time into succession problems.

Phase 1 begins with the most visible dimension: brand consistency. Not whether your logo is correct. Whether the impression your business creates is the same regardless of who encounters it, where, or when.

Audit 1 Context
Why This Audit Matters

Brand consistency is not a marketing concern. It is a commercial one.

In commodity trading, a 1–2% margin difference on significant revenue is permanent annual income. That margin difference is driven largely by how buyers perceive your positioning — whether they see you as a reliable, premium counterparty or as one of many interchangeable options. Perception is set by consistency. Inconsistent positioning produces interchangeable perception. Interchangeable perception produces commodity pricing.

This audit does not ask whether your branding is beautiful. It asks whether it is consistent — and whether that consistency creates the commercial impression your business deserves.

Audit 1 Diagnosis
Brand Consistency

Eight dimensions of brand consistency — visual identity coherence across touchpoints, messaging consistency in written communications, tone alignment across staff, impression equivalence between in-person and digital presence, proposal and document quality standard, first impression versus ongoing impression gap, partner and supplier perception alignment, and brand standard documentation existence.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Your business creates a different impression depending on who encounters it and how. A customer who meets you in person receives a different experience than one who visits your website. A supplier who reads your proposal sees a different business than a partner who attends a meeting. There is no single, coherent impression that travels consistently — which means your brand is not working as a commercial asset. It is working as a variable that undermines pricing power on every transaction.

What to do this week

Conduct a consistency audit across three touchpoints: your website, your most recent proposal or quotation document, and your most recent email correspondence with a client. Ask one question about each: does this create the same impression? If the answer is no — or uncertain — you have identified your brand consistency gap.

Within 14 days

Define your brand standard in writing. Three sentences: what does your business stand for? Who is it for? What impression should every interaction create? This definition does not need to be sophisticated. It needs to be written down and shared with everyone who represents the business.

Within 30 days

Apply the brand standard to your three highest-frequency touchpoints. Your email signature. Your quotation template. Your website homepage. Consistency at the highest-frequency points produces the most immediate improvement in commercial perception.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Some consistency exists. Your business creates a recognizable impression in certain contexts. The gap is in the touchpoints where the standard drops — the informal communications, the secondary documents, the contexts where the business is represented by someone other than you.

What to do this week

Identify the three touchpoints where your brand consistency is weakest. These are typically staff communications, secondary documents, and digital presence. For each: what is the gap between the impression created and the impression you intend? That gap is your brand consistency correction priority.

Within 14 days

Create templates for your highest-frequency inconsistent touchpoints. Not elaborate brand guidelines — usable templates that produce a consistent impression without requiring individual judgment at each use.

Within 30 days

Test your consistency from the outside. Ask someone unfamiliar with your business to interact with five of your touchpoints and describe the impression they form. Compare their description to your intended impression. The gap is your brand consistency correction target.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Your brand is largely consistent. The impression your business creates travels reliably across contexts. The opportunity at this level is in precision — identifying the specific touchpoints where consistency is weakest and closing those gaps before moving to the positioning and pricing authority that Phase 2 examines.

What to do now

Document your brand standard formally. A written standard that exists only in your head is fragile — it cannot be enforced, taught, or maintained as the business grows. Formalizing it converts brand consistency from a personal achievement into a business system.
