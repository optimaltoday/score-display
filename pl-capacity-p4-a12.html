Audit 12 Context
Why This Audit Matters

Eleven dimensions of professional capacity have been examined individually. The final audit examines them as a system. Structural coherence means that all dimensions of the CAPACITY Pillar are aligned and mutually reinforcing — time architecture that protects high-value work, energy management that fuels it, attention allocation that focuses it, measured capacity that sizes it, boundaries that defend it, recovery that sustains it, patterns that no longer undermine it, role clarity that concentrates it, throughput that converts it, prescription that develops it, and sustainability that ensures it endures.

Where those dimensions are misaligned, the result is structural leakage that limits total professional output regardless of how well any individual dimension performs. This audit maps that coherence — or its absence.

Audit 12 Diagnosis
Capacity Coherence Review

Eight dimensions of capacity coherence — cross-dimension alignment, mutual reinforcement assessment, system integration quality, incoherence identification, combined output potential, architecture completeness, pillar coherence mapping, and structural optimisation readiness.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

The CAPACITY Pillar architecture is not yet functioning as a coherent system. Individual dimensions have been examined but not integrated. Time may be partially protected but energy is not managed to match it. Boundaries may be partially defined but recovery is not sufficient to sustain the output those boundaries were designed to enable.

Structural incoherence of this kind produces a specific and measurable loss: the total output of the professional function is limited not by the weakest individual dimension in isolation, but by the misalignment between dimensions. A chain of mutually reinforcing structures produces exponentially more than the same structures operating independently. The Futureproof™ Diagnostic is designed precisely to identify the specific coherence gaps and sequence the interventions that close them.

What to do this week

Map the twelve CAPACITY dimensions against each other in pairs. For each pairing, ask: does strength in one dimension reinforce strength in the other — or does a gap in one undermine the other? The pairings that show the most significant mutual undermining are your highest-leverage coherence targets. Improvement in one dimension will produce improvement across multiple others simultaneously.

Within 14 days

Identify the single dimension of the CAPACITY Pillar where structural improvement would produce the largest cascade of improvement across other dimensions. This is your coherence leverage point — the architectural intervention that, when made, causes the most other dimensions to improve as a consequence. Focus all structural energy on that one point first.

Within 30 days

Implement the coherence leverage point intervention. At 30 days, assess not just the improvement in that dimension but the improvement in the dimensions it was identified as reinforcing. The cascade effect is your evidence that the architectural coherence work is producing structural rather than symptomatic improvement.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

The remaining incoherence is in the integration — the specific points where addressed dimensions are not yet reinforcing each other, where a gap in one dimension is limiting the output of an adjacent addressed one, and where the potential of the improved architecture is not being fully realised because the system is not yet integrated.

What to do this week

Identify the two most significant coherence gaps remaining in your CAPACITY architecture — the two points where a dimension you have improved is being partially undermined by an adjacent dimension that has not yet been addressed. These gaps are the structural source of the performance ceiling you have reached: they prevent the improved dimensions from producing their full potential.

Within 14 days

For the highest-cost coherence gap: what is the specific structural intervention that would close it? Not an improvement to either individual dimension — the specific architectural connection that integrates them. That integration is a different and more precise intervention than improving either dimension independently.

Within 30 days

Implement the coherence integration for your highest-cost gap. At 30 days, assess the change in total output potential — not in any one dimension, but in the combined output of the integrated architecture. Coherence improvement is always felt as a whole-system shift, not as improvement in a single component.
