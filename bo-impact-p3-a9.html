Audits 7 and 8 examined digital presence and communication consistency — the structural conditions that either maintain or undermine positioning within known relationships. Audit 9 examines geographic authority — the reach of your positioning beyond your immediate market.

The capabilities that command a strong local reputation have the potential to command a regional or international premium — but only if the positioning reaches the markets where that premium is available.

Audit 9 Context
Why This Audit Matters

Geographic authority gap is the IMPACT Pillar's most direct money left measurement.

Most Southern Thai businesses of significant scale are unknown beyond their immediate operating geography. Their capabilities — in rubber trading, food production, logistics, healthcare, or property — are entirely comparable to businesses commanding significantly higher prices in regional and international markets. The price difference is not a capability difference. It is an authority difference. The regional and international markets do not know the business exists, or know it only in a generic, commodity-priced context.

Closing the geographic authority gap converts local expertise into regional and international income — at price points that are structurally unavailable within the local market alone.

Audit 9 Diagnosis
Geographic Authority

Eight dimensions of geographic authority — authority radius current measurement, highest-value market awareness status, international buyer recognition, Singapore capital visibility, regional trade association presence, cross-border referral network depth, media and publication presence in target markets, and authority extension investment.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Your business's authority is geographically concentrated. Outside your immediate market, you are unknown. This means the full commercial value of your capabilities is only extractable at local prices — because the markets that would pay regional or international premiums for equivalent capabilities do not know you exist.

The gap between local pricing and regional pricing for comparable capabilities in ASEAN commodity-adjacent businesses is routinely 15–30%. At significant revenue, that gap represents permanent annual income accessible only through geographic authority extension.

What to do this week

Map your authority gap precisely. Which specific markets — Singapore buyers, Malaysian partners, international commodity traders, regional healthcare networks — represent the highest-value commercial opportunities for your capabilities? For each: what is your current visibility there? The gap between potential and current visibility is your geographic authority correction target.

Within 14 days

Identify one authority extension action for your highest-priority target market. Not a comprehensive market entry program — one specific action: a presentation at a regional trade event, a published article in a sector publication, a structured introduction to a Singapore connector. Authority extends one relationship and one interaction at a time.

Within 30 days

Join one regional or international professional body or trade association relevant to your sector. Membership alone does not build authority. Active participation does — and active participation in the right associations puts your business in front of the buyers, investors, and partners who represent your geographic authority gap.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Some geographic authority exists beyond your immediate market. Certain regional relationships and recognitions are in place. The gap is in the specific markets where your highest-value commercial opportunities are located — typically Singapore capital networks and international buyer relationships — which remain underserved by your current authority reach.

What to do this week

Audit the quality of your cross-border relationships. For each relationship outside your immediate market: does this relationship have active commercial potential? Is it maintained actively? Does the contact have visibility into the commercial opportunities your business is positioned to pursue? Relationships that are not actively maintained are not authority assets — they are dormant contacts.

Within 14 days

Identify your strongest existing cross-border relationship and deepen it deliberately. Not with a transactional request — with a valuable share: market intelligence, a relevant introduction, a piece of analysis. Authority in new geographies is built through demonstrated value, not through presence alone.

Within 30 days

Design a geographic authority extension plan for one target market. Three actions, three months, one specific commercial objective. Small and specific plans produce results. Large and comprehensive plans produce intentions.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Your geographic authority extends meaningfully beyond your immediate market. You are recognized in regional and international contexts that are commercially relevant to your business. The opportunity at this level is in conversion — ensuring that geographic authority translates into geographic revenue, not just geographic recognition.

What to do now

Map the commercial conversion rate of your geographic authority. For each market where you have established recognition: what commercial outcomes has that recognition produced? What is the gap between the recognition and the revenue? Closing that gap — from known to engaged to commercial — is the Phase 4 priority for the geographic authority dimension.
