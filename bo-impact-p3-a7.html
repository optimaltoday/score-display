Phase 3 Context
Why This Matters Now

Phase 3 is Diagnosis. This phase examines the structural causes beneath the commercial brand gaps measured in Phase 2.

The IMPACT Pillar's Phase 2 audits revealed the commercial cost of positioning and authority gaps. Phase 3 identifies the structural conditions that produce and maintain those gaps. Digital presence is the first structural examination — because in 2026, a potential client's first impression of your business is almost always digital, and a digital impression that does not match your actual positioning creates a conversion barrier before the relationship has begun.

Audit 7 Context
Why This Audit Matters

Your digital presence is your first impression for most potential high-value clients.

Before a client agrees to meet you, before a partner considers engaging you, before a Singapore investor considers your proposal — they will search for your business digitally. What they find in that search establishes the impression against which every subsequent interaction is measured. A digital presence that understates your actual positioning means every client meeting begins with a credibility rebuilding exercise rather than a credibility confirmation.

For Southern Thai and ASEAN family businesses seeking Singapore capital, international buyers, or premium clients, this gap is particularly costly. The international capital markets that this Pillar is designed to make accessible make their initial assessment digitally. A digital presence that is provincial, outdated, or inconsistent with a premium positioning eliminates the business from consideration before the conversation begins.

Audit 7 Diagnosis
Digital Presence

Eight dimensions of digital presence quality — website positioning alignment, social profile coherence, digital document quality, search result impression management, digital asset currency, online review alignment with positioning, digital communication tone consistency, and digital first-impression quality assessment.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

Your digital presence significantly understates your actual positioning. Potential clients who search for your business before meeting you arrive with an impression that is less premium, less specific, and less commercially compelling than what your in-person reality delivers. Every meeting that follows must first correct that impression — consuming trust-building time and creating a credibility gap that a coherent digital presence would prevent.

What to do this week

Search for your business as a potential client would. Use your business name, your sector, and your location. Examine every result on the first two pages. For each result: does this create the impression you intend? Does it communicate your differentiation? Is it current? The answer across those results is your digital presence reality.

Within 14 days

Update your highest-traffic digital asset to match your current positioning. This is typically your website homepage or your Google Business Profile. One updated asset — fully aligned with your current positioning — is more valuable than a comprehensive but unfocused digital overhaul.

Within 30 days

Audit your digital documents — proposals, capability statements, presentations — for positioning alignment. These are your highest-stakes digital touchpoints because they are received by people actively evaluating you. A document that does not reflect your current positioning is a positioning liability in the hands of a prospective client.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Some digital assets are strong and well-aligned. Others create impressions that do not match your intended positioning. The gap is in coverage — the secondary assets that are seen by more people than you realize and create impressions that partially undermine your primary positioning.

What to do this week

Audit your secondary digital assets — social profiles, directory listings, industry association pages. These are frequently the first digital encounter for clients who find you through a search rather than a direct referral. Misaligned secondary assets create confusion before the primary relationship has a chance to establish the correct impression.

Within 14 days

Create a digital asset inventory. List every digital touchpoint where your business appears. Assign each a quality rating: aligned, partially aligned, or misaligned with your current positioning. Focus correction effort on the misaligned assets that receive the most traffic.

Within 30 days

Establish a digital asset review cadence. Quarterly, review your highest-traffic digital assets for positioning currency. Digital assets decay — not because they are changed, but because the business develops while the assets remain static. Regular review prevents the gap from widening.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Your digital presence is largely coherent with your positioning. The impression potential clients form online matches the impression your in-person reality delivers. The opportunity at this level is in amplification — extending your digital presence into the channels where your target clients are actively looking, and ensuring your positioning is visible where international capital and premium buyers search.

What to do now

Map the digital channels where your highest-value prospective clients search. If Singapore family offices and international buyers are your target, LinkedIn and international trade directories are more commercially important than local social media. Allocate digital presence investment to the channels your most valuable clients actually use.
