Audit 8 Context
Why This Audit Matters

Opportunity is not randomly distributed. It surfaces through specific channels, in specific contexts, via specific types of professional relationship. Most professionals have a structural pattern of opportunity sourcing that reflects the channels, contexts, and relationships of their professional past — which means the opportunity pipeline often reflects the professional conditions of three to five years ago rather than the full range available to the current professional position.

This audit examines the structural patterns governing opportunity identification and evaluation — and where those patterns may be creating structural blindness to the highest-value opportunities available.

Audit 8 Diagnosis
Opportunity Patterns

Eight dimensions of opportunity patterns — opportunity source diversity, evaluation framework consistency, selection bias identification, opportunity pipeline quality, missed opportunity pattern, opportunity development behaviour, structural blindness areas, and opportunity conversion rate.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

The structural patterns governing professional opportunity have not been examined. The opportunities that appear in the professional field of view are the natural outcome of existing relationships, established channels, and historical professional context — not the output of a deliberate architecture designed to surface the full range of opportunity the current professional position and expertise would support.

The structural consequence is systematic blindness to opportunity categories outside the existing pattern. High-value opportunities in adjacent sectors, via relationships not yet built, through channels not historically used, or in professional contexts not previously engaged — all of these are outside the pattern and therefore structurally invisible. The professional operating within an unexamined opportunity pattern is seeing only the portion of the available opportunity landscape that the pattern makes visible.

What to do this week

Map your last 12 months of professional opportunities: every significant engagement, contract, project, or advancement that was presented for consideration. For each: through what channel did it surface? From what type of relationship? In what professional context? The pattern of the sourcing — the consistent channels, relationships, and contexts from which opportunities are arriving — is your opportunity architecture baseline. It tells you what the pattern is currently making visible and, by implication, what it is not.

Within 14 days

Identify the most significant opportunity category that is absent from your current pipeline — the type of professional engagement, at the level your expertise supports, that should be present but is not. The absence is the structural signal. What would need to be true — about channels, relationships, or contexts — for that category of opportunity to begin appearing?

Within 30 days

Build one structural change that would begin to surface opportunities in the absent category: a new channel engagement, a deliberate relationship with a specific type of professional connection, or a presence in a specific professional context where the absent opportunity category most frequently originates. One structural change begins the architecture.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Some opportunity patterns are recognised. The sourcing channels, the evaluation tendencies, and the selection biases are partially visible. The structural gap is in the completeness of the examination — the patterns that are not yet visible are the ones most likely to be producing the most significant structural opportunity blindness.

What to do this week

Apply the evaluation bias test to your most recent three significant opportunity decisions. For each: what was the primary evaluation criterion — fit, compensation, growth potential, relationship quality? Is the same criterion appearing in each evaluation? A consistent primary criterion is a structural bias — it means that opportunities that score low on that criterion are being systematically deprioritised regardless of their value on other dimensions.

Within 14 days

Identify the highest-value dimension on which your current opportunity evaluation systematically underweights. What type of professional opportunity would score highly on that dimension but is being consistently deprioritised by the current evaluation pattern? That opportunity type is your structural bias correction target.

Within 30 days

Deliberately evaluate one opportunity from the underweighted dimension as the primary criterion rather than the secondary one. The result — whether the opportunity looks different when evaluated primarily from the underweighted dimension — is the evidence of whether the evaluation bias is producing structural opportunity misallocation.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

Opportunity patterns are well-examined and the most significant structural biases have been addressed. The opportunity pipeline reflects a broad and deliberately constructed range of professional potential. The opportunity at this level is in opportunity architecture proactivity — moving from a well-examined reactive opportunity pattern to a proactively designed opportunity sourcing strategy that consistently surfaces the highest-value opportunities before they become widely visible.

What to do now

Design one proactive opportunity sourcing mechanism: a specific action or relationship or context engagement that is designed to surface a category of professional opportunity that does not currently appear reliably in your pipeline. The mechanism should be structural — repeating and reliable — rather than a one-time exploratory action. Proactive opportunity architecture generates consistent return; exploratory actions generate occasional insight.
