Audit 5 Context
Why This Audit Matters

A methodology is not a process. A process describes what steps are taken. A methodology explains why those steps produce results that other approaches do not — it is a theory of change encoded in a repeatable structure. A named, documented, and owned methodology is the highest-leverage form of expertise protection: it makes your professional approach structurally distinct, difficult to replicate without attribution, and commercially communicable in a way that raw expertise cannot be.

Most professionals have the raw material for a proprietary methodology and have never formalised it. This audit examines whether that formalisation has occurred — and if not, what it would take to make it happen.

Audit 5 Diagnosis
Methodology Protection

Eight dimensions of methodology protection — proprietary framework existence, naming and branding, documentation completeness, intellectual property consciousness, market recognition, commercial deployment, differentiation clarity, and methodology communication.

If you scored Losing  ·  8–18

No proprietary methodology has been articulated. Your professional approach may be distinctive — built from years of refined practice, developed through pattern recognition across hundreds of engagements — but it has not been named, documented, or positioned as structurally yours. It exists in practice but not as intellectual property.

The structural consequence is replaceability. A professional without a named methodology is a professional whose approach can be described generically by competitors and substituted with alternatives that appear equivalent — because no structural architecture distinguishes what they deliver. The expertise that differentiates you is invisible in the market because it has never been made visible.

What to do this week

Name your approach. This week, write one sentence that describes the distinctive logic of how you produce results that others do not — not what you do, but why your approach produces a different outcome. This is the seed of your methodology statement. It does not need to be polished. It needs to be honest about what makes your professional practice structurally different.

Within 14 days

Build the architecture of your methodology: what is the problem it is designed to solve, what are the stages it moves through, what does each stage produce, and what is the underlying principle that makes it work? This is a framework, not a process — you are capturing the logic, not the task list.

Within 30 days

Name the methodology formally. A name does not need to be clever — it needs to be clear, ownable, and memorable in your professional context. A named methodology is an intellectual property asset. Begin using it consistently in client communications, proposals, and professional introductions.

If you scored Leaking  ·  19–29

Proprietary approaches exist but are not formalised. You use distinctive frameworks, ask distinctive questions, and produce results through a distinctive reasoning architecture — but none of it has been named, documented, or positioned as intellectual property. Clients and colleagues may recognise that you approach problems differently; they cannot describe what that difference is in structural terms, because you have not given them the architecture to do so.

What to do this week

Inventory your existing frameworks and approaches. List every distinctive tool, question sequence, diagnostic lens, or decision structure you use. For each: could another professional adopt this approach if they observed it closely? If yes, it is not yet protected. If no — if there is something in the reasoning architecture that is difficult to replicate without deep understanding of your professional logic — that is the core of your proprietary methodology.

Within 14 days

Consolidate the most distinctive elements of your existing approaches into one named methodology. It does not need to encompass everything you do — it needs to accurately represent the most differentiated part of your professional approach. A methodology that captures 60% of your distinctiveness and is named is more commercially valuable than one that captures 100% but exists only in your practice.

Within 30 days

Begin positioning the named methodology in one professional context: a proposal, a presentation, a professional introduction, or a published piece. The first time a potential client or peer refers back to your methodology by name is the structural evidence that it is functioning as an intellectual property asset.

If you scored Leveraging  ·  30–40

A proprietary methodology exists, is named, and is positioned. It functions as a commercial differentiator. The opportunity at this level is in methodology leverage — whether the methodology is being systematically deployed across all relevant professional touchpoints, and whether its intellectual property status is being protected with the rigour that its commercial value warrants.

What to do now

Assess the protection architecture around your methodology. Is the name trademarked or registered where appropriate for your jurisdiction and market? Is it documented in a form that would establish prior art if the approach were adopted by others? Is it consistently positioned across your professional materials? Methodology leverage requires protection — a named framework with no protection architecture is a valuable asset with an unlocked door.
